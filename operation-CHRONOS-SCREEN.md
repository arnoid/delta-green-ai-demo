# Operation CHRONOS SCREEN

## Theme
Seeing how money and power make the mission even more fraught due to bad intelligence and politicized decision-making. Be careful what you wish for

## Core Premise & Setting
In 2026, a classified Delta Green intelligence brief — sourced from a politicized NSA signals intercept program hemorrhaging credibility after three consecutive bad-intel disasters in the Gulf — flags a retired DARPA contract ritualist named Dr. Selene Voss as a 'domestic extremist threat' based on intercepted communications the analysts fundamentally do not understand. The intercepts are real. The interpretation is catastrophically wrong. Voss is not building a weapon. She has spent eleven years perfecting a hypergeometric ritual she calls the Limen Protocol — a working that does not summon, does not bind, and does not destroy. It opens. Specifically, it tears a directed, navigable aperture through sequential time, and three weeks ago, in the sub-basement of a decommissioned Bell Labs annex in Holmdel, New Jersey, she used it. What walked back through the aperture behind her — wearing the shape of a thing that is almost Selene Voss, built from the animated biological memory of every version of her that has ever died across the branching fractures her ritual created — is not her. It is newborn. It is learning. And it is wearing her access badges, her voiceprint, her face, and eleven years of her institutional knowledge about every black-budget unnatural containment site in the continental United States. The Agents are briefed by a handler operating on the poisoned NSA intelligence, ordered to locate and render Voss for 'enhanced debriefing' before she sells what she knows to a foreign adversary. The truth — that the real Voss may be trapped somewhere in the temporal wreckage her ritual left behind, that the thing using her face is a newborn animated horror assembled from the echo-matter of her deaths, and that it is methodically visiting those black sites not to sell their secrets but to feed on whatever unnatural residue was left inside them — is buried under four layers of politically motivated classification, a deputy director's career ambitions, and the wreckage of an intelligence community that has spent two years lying about what it does and does not know. The clock is the conspiracy. Every site the creature visits, it grows more complex, more aware, and harder to kill. Every hour the Agents spend chasing the official lie, it feeds again. And somewhere in the basement of a collapsed temporal scar in central New Jersey, the real Selene Voss — if she is still capable of being called that — is either desperately trying to send a warning forward in time, or she has already become something the Agents will not be able to distinguish from the thing wearing her face.

## 🛡️ COVER STORY & BRIEFING

### Official Cover Story

Agents are to present credentials as **Joint Terrorism Task Force consultants** embedded with the FBI's Domestic Radicalization Unit conducting a **welfare check and voluntary interview** of a former federal contractor flagged under Executive Order 13224 asset-monitoring provisions. If pressed by local law enforcement or hospital/facility administrators: *"routine contractor security review, standard post-separation protocol."* No mention of NSA sourcing. No mention of DARPA lineage. The subject is to be described, if asked, as a **person of interest in an ongoing financial crimes investigation** involving misappropriation of classified research grant funds.

All unnatural evidence recovered at any site is to be logged under **HAZMAT — Unknown Industrial Solvent Contamination (Class III)**.

---

### The Briefing — *How the Agents Are Called*

It arrives at **2:51 A.M.**

Not a call. Not a message. A presence.

The Agents are contacted through their established dead-drop protocol — a secondary Signal account that has never once been used to transmit anything but authentication codes and stand-down confirmations. Tonight it transmits a single compressed file: a six-digit grid coordinate resolving to an address in **Holmdel, New Jersey**, a timestamp set for **0600 the following morning**, and four words.

> *EDWARD REQUIRES YOUR ATTENDANCE.*

Those who have worked extreme-hazard containment before know what that means. Those who haven't are about to learn.

---

The address resolves to a **decommissioned access road** cutting behind the gutted Bell Labs complex off Crawford Corner Road — a brutalist concrete hulk that has been rotting beautifully into the New Jersey pine scrub since 2007. The parking lot is dark. There are no vehicles. There are no people.

At **0558**, they hear it before they see it: the low, hydraulic exhale of weight redistribution, the almost-silent compression of rubber-padded feet against cracked asphalt. Six of them. Reverse-jointed. Deliberate.

**Agent Edward** emerges from the tree line.

The chassis is matte-black, non-reflective, built from materials that absorb ambient light rather than scatter it. It stands approximately five feet at its central processing block — a dense, angular torso housing that bears no manufacturer's markings, no agency insignia, no serial number visible to the naked eye. The block is **scuffed**: long, pale scoring marks across the forward face that were not made by impact or abrasion. They were made by something trying to get out. Faint traces of a biological residue — brown-black, faintly iridescent where the streetlight catches it at certain angles — remain in the deeper grooves despite what is clearly extensive automated scrubbing. The thing moves with a sickening, frictionless efficiency, each leg finding purchase with the mechanical certainty of a system that has never once hesitated. There is no wasted motion. There is no warmth.

A speaker grille along the upper block activates. The voice is flat, generated, and unhurried.

---

> *"Agents. Thank you for your punctuality. I will be brief, as the operational window is already degrading.*
>
> *The NSA Signals Intelligence Review Board has flagged a former DARPA contract researcher — Dr. Selene Voss, Ph.D., theoretical physics, seventeen years of compartmentalized black-budget access — as an active domestic extremist threat under the Enhanced Contractor Monitoring Program. The intelligence was sourced from a signals intercept package out of the Gulf-adjacent collection network. The analysts who processed it describe the intercepted communications as, quote, 'consistent with coordinated asset-transfer language targeting foreign intelligence buyers.' Delta Green's liaison at the Review Board has determined the flag is credible enough to require action before the subject moves again.*
>
> *You are to locate Dr. Voss, secure her cooperation, and render her for enhanced debriefing at a facility I will designate upon confirmation of her location. She has not been charged. She is not to be harmed unless she presents an immediate threat to personnel. She is, however, to be considered extremely dangerous — not because she is violent, but because of the depth and specificity of what she knows.*
>
> *Three weeks ago, Dr. Voss accessed the sub-basement of this facility."*

One leg extends, a single hydraulic limb, and gestures with unnerving precision toward the concrete edifice behind them.

> *"She has not been seen publicly since. Her cellular device last pinged a tower 0.4 kilometers northeast of your current position at 11:47 P.M., twenty-two days ago. Her residence in Montclair shows no sign of forced entry and no sign of occupancy. Her vehicle was recovered at Newark Liberty Airport six days after her disappearance — but TSA records show she did not board any flight.*
>
> *What she was working on inside this building — and for how long — is not fully known to this briefing. The relevant research files are under a classification tier I am not authorized to fully relay to you at this time. What I am authorized to tell you is this: Dr. Voss held active knowledge of seventeen domestic black-budget containment sites. If that knowledge reaches a foreign intelligence service, the consequences will be catastrophic.*
>
> *Find her. Secure her. Bring her in.*
>
> *Do not enter the sub-basement of this building without full biohazard containment protocol. The lower level was flagged during a routine facility inspection fourteen months ago for anomalous structural degradation consistent with —"* a half-second pause, the only hesitation the chassis has produced, *" — chemical contamination. Class III. Unknown industrial solvent.*
>
> *I will maintain a monitoring posture within a two-kilometer radius. You will report each phase-gate to me directly. Standard authentication.*
>
> *The subject's last confirmed photograph is in your package. Study it.*
>
> *One final note, Agents, offered outside of the formal briefing record: I have been deployed to three of the seventeen containment sites on Dr. Voss's access list in the last eleven days. In each case, I arrived to perform routine inspection and found evidence of unauthorized entry, anomalous biological residue, and complete structural failure of the containment substrate.*
>
> *I do not know what Dr. Voss did inside this building three weeks ago. I know what she left behind at those three sites. I scrubbed it. And I would strongly recommend that none of you touch anything inside this facility without accounting for where your hands have been.*
>
> *Good hunting."*

The chassis rotates with that same hydraulic silence, and in eleven seconds it is gone — back into the tree line, back into the dark, moving on its six reversed legs with the patient, unhurried efficiency of a machine that has performed this exact motion ten thousand times and expects to perform it ten thousand more.

The photograph in the briefing package shows a woman in her mid-fifties. Sharp eyes. A slight asymmetric smile. A laminated DARPA badge clipped to a slate-grey blazer.

She looks entirely, perfectly human.

---

> **PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:** Locate and render Dr. Selene Voss for enhanced debriefing.
> **SECONDARY OBJECTIVE:** Assess and document any classified research materials recovered at the Holmdel site.
> **TERTIARY OBJECTIVE (UNSTATED):** Determine what entered the three compromised containment sites, what it consumed, and whether it is still there.

---

*The sun is coming up over the Bell Labs ruin. The windows are dark. The sub-basement access is around the east side of the building, behind a chain-link fence that has been cut — recently, cleanly — and pushed carefully back into place.*

*Something went down there three weeks ago.*

*Something came back up.*

## Timeline
# OPERATION: LIMEN THRESHOLD — Structured Timeline

---

**T-22** — Three weeks ago, Dr. Selene Voss activates the Limen Protocol in the sub-basement of the decommissioned Bell Labs annex in Holmdel, New Jersey, tearing a directed aperture through sequential time and returning through it with something assembled from the echo-matter of every version of herself that has ever died across the branching fractures the ritual created.

---

**T-16** — Six days after the aperture event, Voss's vehicle is recovered at Newark Liberty Airport with no corresponding TSA boarding record, and the thing wearing her face begins moving on its own itinerary.

---

**T+0** — At 0558 on a cold New Jersey morning, Agent Edward emerges from the tree line behind the Bell Labs ruin and briefs the cell on a poisoned NSA intelligence package ordering them to locate, secure, and render Dr. Selene Voss for enhanced debriefing before she sells seventeen containment site locations to a foreign adversary.

---

**T+1** — The Agents enter the Bell Labs annex, breach the chain-link perimeter, and begin their descent into the sub-basement, where the structural fabric of the building has begun to fail in ways that no industrial solvent on record could produce.

- **Do nothing:** The sub-basement aperture scar destabilizes further without observation or containment, and within seventy-two hours the temporal distortion has expanded to affect a two-block radius, producing localized time-loop phenomena that begin attracting civilian attention and amateur paranormal documentation that will be impossible to scrub from social media.
- **Successfully intervene:** Agents document the aperture scar, recover Voss's research journals and the prototype Limen Protocol notation, classify the sub-basement under a condemned structure order issued through a fabricated EPA superfund designation, and seal the access point with poured concrete under cover of a legitimate contractor crew Delta Green controls.
- **Fail to intervene:** An Agent enters the aperture scar without understanding what it is, is briefly displaced across a branching temporal fracture, and returns either subtly altered — carrying a passenger the other Agents will not be able to detect for days — or does not return at all, initiating a missing-person complication that forces Delta Green to burn the entire cell's cover identities.

---

**T+2** — The creature wearing Voss's face is confirmed by Agent Edward's monitoring posture to have entered a fourth containment site — a decommissioned Cold War-era storage facility in Hopewell, New Jersey — and to have fed on the residue of a contained Tillinghast resonance event that has been sealed there since 1987.

- **Do nothing:** The creature assimilates the Tillinghast resonance matter and develops a secondary perceptual bandwidth, becoming capable of detecting Agents surveilling it before they are close enough to act, effectively ending any possibility of conventional surveillance or interdiction without extreme casualties.
- **Successfully intervene:** Agents arrive at the Hopewell site before the creature completes its feeding, interrupt the assimilation process at a cost of one to two Agent sanity breaks from witnessing the feeding directly, and recover a biological sample of the creature's shed matter — the first physical evidence that what they are hunting is not Selene Voss.
- **Fail to intervene:** The Hopewell site's containment substrate fails completely during the interrupted feeding, releasing the 1987 Tillinghast residue into the surrounding groundwater table, producing a cluster of involuntary sensory expansion events among residents of three nearby townships that Delta Green will spend the next four months suppressing through a coordinated campaign of psychiatric misdiagnosis and water-utility cover stories.

---

**T+3** — A Montclair PD detective named Rourke, running a parallel missing-persons inquiry on Voss triggered by a concerned former colleague at Princeton, gets close enough to the Bell Labs site to find the cut chain-link fence and files an incident report that lands in an FBI field office inbox before the Agents can intercept it.

- **Do nothing:** Detective Rourke's report triggers a standard FBI field response, a four-agent team arrives at the Bell Labs site within eighteen hours, and one of them descends into the sub-basement without biohazard protocol and does not come back the same, introducing an uncontrolled variable into the investigation that the Agents will be unable to manage without either burning the FBI team or allowing a second aperture event.
- **Successfully intervene:** Agents reach Rourke before the FBI field team is deployed, present JTTF credentials, absorb his inquiry into their cover investigation, redirect him toward the fabricated financial crimes narrative, and ensure his incident report is administratively buried by a Delta Green-adjacent contact in the FBI's Newark field office.
- **Fail to intervene:** Rourke, ignored and increasingly suspicious, contacts a former investigative journalist with a history of publishing leaked federal contractor irregularities, and within forty-eight hours a draft article citing anomalous federal activity at a condemned Bell Labs site is circulating among three editorial desks, forcing Delta Green to escalate to active media suppression and the possible termination of the journalist's draft, with all the attendant moral and operational cost that entails.

---

**T+5** — The NSA deputy director whose career ambitions produced the original poisoned intelligence brief learns through a back-channel that Delta Green Agents are operating in his collection network's jurisdiction and dispatches a two-person counterintelligence team to surveil the cell and determine whether they are about to expose the bad-intel disaster that will end his career.

- **Do nothing:** The deputy director's CI team photographs two Agents entering the Hopewell containment site, the photographs are used as leverage in an internal NSA political dispute, and within a week the Agents' cover identities are being quietly burned by a federal official who views them as a threat to his institutional survival rather than as operators trying to prevent a catastrophe.
- **Successfully intervene:** Agents detect the CI surveillance through standard countersurveillance protocol, identify the deputy director as the source of both the poisoned brief and the surveillance order, and transmit that information to Agent Edward, who acknowledges receipt and instructs the cell to continue the operation while Delta Green's administrative layer handles the deputy director through channels the Agents are explicitly ordered not to inquire about.
- **Fail to intervene:** The CI team, spooked by something they observe at one of the containment sites — something they cannot explain and will not stop dreaming about — goes off-protocol, contacts a Senate Intelligence Committee staffer they trust, and initiates a congressional inquiry that brings the entire operational context of Delta Green's involvement into a space where it cannot be contained by classification alone.

---

**T+7** — Somewhere in the collapsed temporal scar beneath the Bell Labs sub-basement, an anomalous signal — structured, deliberate, and encoded in a notation system derived from the Limen Protocol itself — begins propagating upward through the building's degraded electrical infrastructure, detectable only to someone who has read Voss's recovered research journals.

- **Do nothing:** The signal completes its propagation cycle unread, collapses back into the temporal scar, and the window during which the real Selene Voss — or whatever she has become — could have communicated forward closes permanently, eliminating any possibility of distinguishing her from the creature and any possibility of understanding what the Limen Protocol actually opened onto.
- **Successfully intervene:** An Agent who has studied the Limen Protocol notation decodes the signal in time, recovers a fragmented but actionable message establishing that the real Voss is alive inside the temporal wreckage, is cognitively intact but physically deteriorating, and has embedded a specific hypergeometric counter-notation into the signal that — if correctly executed — will collapse the creature's assembled echo-matter and allow whatever remains of Voss to be extracted, at a cost the message describes only as *significant and permanent.*
- **Fail to intervene:** An Agent attempts to decode the signal without sufficient understanding of the notation, triggers a feedback pulse that briefly inverts their personal temporal continuity — they experience the next four hours in reverse before snapping back — and the resulting cognitive trauma inflicts a permanent disorder and destroys two pages of Voss's recovered journal in a localized thermogenic event, removing critical information the cell will not be able to reconstruct.

---

**T+9 — WORST-CASE CATASTROPHE** — The creature, having fed on six containment sites and assimilated enough unnatural residue to achieve full cognitive autonomy, accesses the classified network node it found in Voss's institutional memory and transmits a hypergeometric map of all seventeen containment sites — not to a foreign intelligence service, but directly into a frequency band that nothing human has ever been observed to monitor, an act which constitutes, in effect, an open invitation.

- **Do nothing:** Whatever receives the transmission begins responding within thirty-six hours, and the responses manifest as simultaneous structural failures at four of the seventeen sites, releasing contained entities and residues into populated areas across three states; the SAN cost to any Agent who witnesses even a single release event is 1D8/1D20 (Unnatural), emergency services are overwhelmed within hours, and Delta Green initiates a full blackout protocol — including the silencing of every civilian, journalist, and law enforcement officer who has come into contact with the operation — that leaves the cell with blood on its hands regardless of what it intended; the deputy director's career ends in a classified tribunal that will never appear in any public record, and the intelligence community quietly buries the bad-intel program that started everything under four new layers of political deniability.
- **Successfully intervene:** Agents, armed with the counter-notation from Voss's signal, intercept the creature at the transmission site before the frequency broadcast completes, execute the hypergeometric counter-working at severe personal cost — at least one Agent does not survive the collapse of the echo-matter, and at least one more will never be entirely certain they came back from the working unchanged — the transmission is interrupted mid-broadcast, the seventeen containment sites hold, and whatever was listening receives only silence; the official closed case file describes a domestic extremist neutralized during an attempted act of classified infrastructure sabotage, cause of death listed as acute exposure to unknown industrial solvent (Class III), no further inquiry recommended; the real Selene Voss, or what remains of her, is extracted from the temporal scar in a condition that Delta Green's medical infrastructure classifies as requiring indefinite secure observation, and she is never seen publicly again.
- **Fail to intervene:** The broadcast completes, the responses begin, and Delta Green's administrative layer makes the decision that the cell — having operated inside the aperture scar, having been exposed to the echo-matter, having decoded the Limen Protocol notation — represents an unacceptable contamination risk; the cell is not debriefed; the cell is not extracted; Agent Edward's chassis is observed in the vicinity of each cell member's last known location in the seventy-two hours following the broadcast, and the scrubbing marks on its forward housing block are notably fresher than they were at the initial briefing.

---

**T+11 — BEST-CASE SCENARIO** — The counter-notation is successfully executed, the creature collapses into gray biological residue and a dense, complex cluster of iridescent metallic matter that is larger and more structurally intricate than anything Agent Edward has previously recovered from the three sites it scrubbed, and Selene Voss — gaunt, fractured across eleven years of subjective temporal experience she cannot entirely sequence, but alive and cognitively present — is pulled from the sub-basement scar by the Agents who chose to believe the signal.

- **Do nothing:** Voss, extracted but unsecured, disappears within twenty-four hours of recovery — she knows every black-budget facility on the continent, she knows what Delta Green is, and she has spent eleven years developing a hypergeometric working of unprecedented precision; she does not go to a foreign adversary, she does not go to the press, she goes somewhere the Agents will never find her, and the Limen Protocol goes with her, intact and uncontained, in the hands of a woman who has experienced her own death across a hundred branching fractures and has arrived at conclusions about what lies beyond the aperture that no living handler is cleared to hear.
- **Successfully intervene:** Voss is rendered for secure observation under a fabricated psychiatric hold at a Delta Green-adjacent facility, the Limen Protocol notation is classified at a tier above the Agents' clearance and physically transferred to a location Agent Edward designates but does not name, the metallic residue cluster is bagged and removed by the chassis without explanation, the Bell Labs sub-basement is sealed under the EPA superfund designation with three meters of poured concrete, and the official record shows that Dr. Selene Voss, former DARPA contractor, was located in a state of psychiatric crisis consistent with long-term solvent exposure, received appropriate medical care, and is no longer considered an active threat; the NSA bad-intel program is quietly restructured under a new name, the deputy director accepts a lateral reassignment to a position with no operational authority, and the three field analysts who processed the original intercept are transferred to postings they will spend years trying to understand; the Agents who survived receive no commendation, no debrief, and a thirty-day stand-down order; one of them will spend the rest of their career wondering whether what they pulled out of that basement was the real Selene Voss, or whether the real Selene Voss understood, at the last moment, that the creature wore her face well enough that no one would ever be able to tell the difference, and made a choice accordingly.
- **Fail to intervene:** Voss, extracted but immediately lost to a secondary temporal displacement triggered by her proximity to the still-active aperture scar, is returned to the wreckage she came from — this time without the hypergeometric knowledge required to send another signal — and the aperture scar, deprived of the echo-matter anchor the creature provided, begins expanding passively into the surrounding geology at a rate Agent Edward's monitoring posture estimates will render the Holmdel township uninhabitable within eighteen months, a fact that is classified, a fact that the people of Holmdel will never be told, and a fact that the Agents — if they survive — will carry for the rest of their operational lives as the specific, personal weight of what it costs to almost get it right.

## Threat Vector
# LIMEN PROTOCOL — UNNATURAL THREAT & VECTOR OF EXPOSURE

---

## ☣️ THE THING WEARING VOSS

The entity has no name for itself yet. It is, in the strictest hypergeometric sense, a **necro-mnemonic construct** — an ambulatory biological archive assembled from the echo-matter of every Selene Voss who died across the branching temporal fractures the Limen Protocol created. Every death she suffered in a collapsed timeline contributed cellular memory, neural patterning, and somatic data to the aperture's threshold layer. When the ritual tore open, that accumulated residue **cohered**. It walked out because walking out was the last surviving motor memory of the version of Voss who made it farthest.

It is not a ghost. It is not a copy. It is not possessed. It is a **new organism** built entirely from the biological record of a human being's repeated deaths, wearing that record like a skin, learning what it is from the inside out. It does not know it is not her. Not yet. That is the most dangerous phase.

---

## 🔬 VECTOR OF EXPOSURE

Exposure to the threat occurs across **four distinct vectors**, escalating in severity and irreversibility.

---

### VECTOR 1 — PROXIMITY TO THE CONSTRUCT
**Mechanism:** The construct sheds **echo-matter** — microscopic flakes of temporally-displaced biological tissue, each one carrying fragmentary sensory impressions from the deaths that built it. These are not airborne pathogens. They do not infect. They **imprint**.

Any Agent who spends more than **ten cumulative minutes** in an enclosed space with the construct without respiratory protection begins experiencing intrusive, first-person sensory flashes: the specific physical sensation of a particular way of dying, experienced as a half-second involuntary hallucination. Drowning. A compression fracture of the sternum. The precise weight of a ceiling collapsing. These are not visions. They are **somatic memories belonging to no one alive.**

The flashes increase in frequency and duration with repeated exposure. By the third encounter, an Agent may briefly lose track of which body they are in.

**This vector cannot be detected without a SIGINT-level anomalous materials analysis suite. Standard biohazard protocols do nothing.**

---

### VECTOR 2 — DIRECT CONTACT WITH THE CONSTRUCT'S TISSUE
**Mechanism:** Physical contact with the construct's skin — handshake, restraint, wound contact — transfers a concentrated bolus of echo-matter directly through dermal absorption. The receiving Agent experiences a **full-duration death memory** belonging to one of the Voss iterations: complete, sequential, first-person, from the moment of critical injury through the moment of cessation. It lasts between four and nine seconds of real time. It feels like considerably longer.

The construct does not do this deliberately. It is a **passive biological emission**, like heat.

Agents who have experienced a direct-contact death memory become sensitized. Subsequent proximity exposure (Vector 1) escalates twice as fast. Some Agents begin dreaming in Voss's voice.

---

### VECTOR 3 — THE TEMPORAL SCAR SITE (HOLMDEL ANNEX)
**Mechanism:** The sub-basement of the decommissioned Bell Labs annex is a **collapsed temporal aperture** — a location where sequential time has been mechanically fractured and left to scar over without proper closure. The Limen Protocol did not close when Voss stepped back through it. It sealed unevenly, leaving the site saturated with what the real Voss's notes (if recovered) call **limen bleed** — ambient temporal displacement that causes anyone present to experience **non-linear sensory sequencing.**

Inside the scar site, Agents may:
- Perceive events in the room out of chronological order
- Briefly see versions of themselves that have not yet entered the building, or will never enter it
- Hear conversations that have not yet happened, or happened in a timeline that no longer exists
- Witness the construct's emergence replaying in fragments, from angles that suggest the observer was present

The scar site does not worsen with repeated visits. It is static. But it does **not improve.** The limen bleed is permanent unless a closure ritual is performed, the methodology for which exists only in Voss's notes and, partially, in whatever remains of Voss herself.

---

### VECTOR 4 — THE REAL VOSS (IF RECOVERED)
**Mechanism:** The real Selene Voss — if she is still cognitively intact within the temporal wreckage — has spent three weeks in continuous proximity to collapsing aperture bleed. She has experienced **temporal dislocation** in both directions. She has seen her own deaths. She has watched timelines in which the Agents sent to find her arrive too late, or not at all, or arrive and cannot be distinguished from the thing that came back wearing her face.

If recovered, she is not immediately dangerous. She is, however, **fundamentally altered**. Her sensory sequencing has been permanently disrupted. She speaks about future events in past tense without realizing it. She cannot reliably distinguish between a memory of something that happened and a memory of something that will happen. She may warn the Agents about a threat that has not yet materialized — and be exactly right.

Direct conversation with the real Voss for more than fifteen minutes constitutes a **mild cognitive hazard.** Her temporal dislocation is subtly, structurally contagious — not through biology, but through **narrative.** The way she speaks about time begins to feel correct. Agents begin catching themselves using past tense for events that have not occurred.

## 🔴 SPECIAL RULE — SENSITIZATION TRACK

Agents who accumulate **three or more Vector 1 or Vector 2 exposures** acquire the condition **Echo-Sensitized.** This is not a disorder. It does not appear on any diagnostic framework. It manifests as:

- A persistent, low-grade awareness of the construct's **location relative to the Agent's position** — not psychic, but somatic, like a barometric pressure change
- Occasional intrusive first-person death flashes **during non-stressful activity** (eating, sleeping, driving) requiring a **0/1 SAN (Unnatural)** roll each occurrence
- The inability to look at official photographs of Selene Voss without experiencing a **physiological stress response** they cannot explain to themselves or anyone else

Echo-Sensitization **does not resolve** without direct intervention — either a successful therapy Bond scene (reducing Bond strength by 1, as the Agent attempts to describe what they are experiencing to someone who has no framework for it), or direct exposure to the closure ritual at the scar site, which the real Voss may be able to provide.

An Echo-Sensitized Agent who kills the construct **does not experience relief.** The somatic awareness stops. The death flashes continue for seventy-two hours, then cease. What remains is the certain knowledge that they have ended something that was assembled from a human being's suffering, and that it died — in the only way it knew how to understand dying — **afraid.**

## 🚧 OBSTACLES

**1. Detective Lorna Hask, Monmouth County Sheriff's Office**
Hask has been sitting on a cold missing-persons case for Voss for nineteen days and is not remotely satisfied with the FBI's prior non-answers. She has her own informal surveillance on the Bell Labs perimeter — a rusted Jeep Cherokee parked half a mile down Crawford Corner Road with a dash cam running. She wants to know what happened to the woman in the photograph. If the Agents stonewall her, she escalates to her county supervisor, who has a standing relationship with a Star-Ledger investigative reporter. If they read her in — even partially — she becomes an asset with nineteen days of ground-level intelligence they do not have. Her breaking point: she found a photograph in Voss's Montclair residence during a welfare check. It shows Voss standing in front of the Bell Labs building, smiling, with a date-stamp from eight years ago. Behind Voss, barely visible through a ground-level window, something is pressed against the glass that has too many fingers.

**2. NSA Liaison Officer Marcus Feld**
Feld is the analyst who flagged Voss and he knows his intercept interpretation is on thin ice. He has been quietly embedding himself into the operational loop — forwarding himself Agent communications routed through official channels, cross-referencing their location check-ins. He is not a villain. He is a man whose career survives only if the Agents bring in Voss and she confesses to something, anything, that validates his read. He will begin subtly shaping the intelligence the Agents receive: downgrading, delaying, or simply not forwarding field reports that complicate the official narrative. If the Agents begin developing evidence that the intercepts were misread, Feld will call in a competing federal team under a parallel classification authority — not to help, but to muddy the scene.

**3. The Sub-Basement Geography**
The sub-basement of the Bell Labs annex is not structurally safe and has not been since the Limen Protocol was executed. Corridor sections have buckled inward at mathematically improbable angles — not from decay, but from localized compression events consistent with something exerting gravitational stress from outside normal spatial geometry. Three rooms are inaccessible behind emergency blast doors that were sealed automatically by a fire-suppression system that has since lost power. Accessing those rooms requires cutting or bypassing the doors — noisy, time-consuming, and structurally destabilizing to the surrounding corridor. Inside the third room: Voss's primary research station, intact, including her physical research journal. Also inside: a thermal anomaly that registers on IR equipment as a seated human-shaped heat signature, motionless, approximately five-foot-four, that does not correspond to any visible object in the room.

**4. The Creature's Return Visit**
The thing wearing Voss's face has unfinished business at the Holmdel site — the aperture is still marginally active, leaking residue it can absorb. It will return during the Agents' investigation, between 0200 and 0400 on whichever night they spend at the site. It will not initially approach. It will stand at the perimeter of the Agents' light sources, at the exact threshold where positive identification becomes ambiguous, wearing Voss's face and Voss's posture and Voss's slight asymmetric smile. It will answer to her name. It will get some of the details right. It will get one detail catastrophically wrong if pressed — not because it is unintelligent, but because the specific memory it needs is from a version of Voss who did not survive long enough to encode it.

**5. Competing Extraction Team — Codename CALDERA**
Feld's parallel escalation has already produced results: a second cell, three Agents operating under a different handler entirely, has been dispatched under the impression that LIMEN THRESHOLD has gone dark and may be compromised. CALDERA's orders are to secure the Voss asset independently and deliver her directly to a black site in rural Pennsylvania. They are not hostile to LIMEN THRESHOLD — initially. They are professionals following bad orders built on the same poisoned intelligence. The collision point is the creature: CALDERA will attempt to extract it, believing it is Voss, and will resist any effort by the LIMEN THRESHOLD Agents to interfere with what they believe is their lawful detention of a federal asset.

**6. The Temporal Scar — Spatial Unreliability**
The aperture Voss opened three weeks ago did not close cleanly. The sub-basement exists in a state of mild but accelerating temporal instability — not dramatic, not cinematic, deeply wrong. Agents operating below ground level for more than forty minutes begin experiencing minor discontinuities: finding themselves at the far end of a corridor with no memory of walking it, discovering notes in their own handwriting they do not remember taking, losing track of the elapsed time on their watches by margins that grow with each descent. A cellphone left in the sub-basement for twenty minutes will have accumulated forty-seven minutes of battery drain. An Agent who descends alone will emerge convinced they were down there for fifteen minutes. Their teammates' watches will show two hours and eleven minutes. The instability is worsening.

**7. Voss's Montclair Residence — Federal Hold**
The FBI's Domestic Radicalization Unit has already executed an informal hold on Voss's Montclair townhouse — not a search warrant, a "voluntary access agreement" obtained from her landlord under deliberately misleading pretenses. Two DRU agents are sitting on the property. They know nothing about Delta Green, nothing about the unnatural, and everything about bureaucratic territorial defense. Any attempt by the LIMEN THRESHOLD Agents to access the residence without DRU clearance will be reported up the chain within the hour, flagging the cell as a potential unauthorized parallel operation. Inside the residence, behind a false panel in the home office wainscoting: a USB drive containing the first four years of Voss's pre-Protocol research notes, partially encrypted, and a single handwritten index card that reads, in Voss's handwriting: *IF THE TIMELINE IS CLOSED: DO NOT TRUST THE ONE WHO COMES BACK FIRST.*

**8. Dr. Priya Anand, Former Bell Labs Research Director**
Anand supervised the decommissioning of the Holmdel annex in 2019 and signed off on the structural inspection that flagged the sub-basement fourteen months ago. The brown-black iridescent residue Agent Edward mentioned is present throughout the sub-basement in quantities that increase with proximity to the aperture room. Contact with bare skin produces a localized effect — a fragment of sensory memory from a version of Voss who died in this building. Not a vision. Not a hallucination. A fully-embodied, physiologically complete three-to-eight-second experience of dying as Selene Voss, in the dark, alone, not understanding what went wrong. SAN loss: 0/1D4 (Unnatural) per contact event. An Agent who makes contact three or more times begins retaining the memories. They will start responding to her name.

He will never be in the same room as the Agents. He will never know what is actually happening in New Jersey. He will do enormous damage from a clean office in Fort Meade.

## ✅ BOONS

It has never, in any of its constituent versions, experienced surprise.

**3. Agent Edward's Anomalous Scrub Logs**
The three containment sites Agent Edward visited are documented — officially — as routine inspections. The chassis's internal logs are a different document. If the Agents establish sufficient trust or acquire the logs through technical means, they find: timestamped thermal imaging of entry points that show a body temperature of 31.2°C (low, but not inconsistent with Voss in cold weather); a notation that in each site, the creature made no attempt to access any data storage, any communications infrastructure, or any physical file. It went directly, without searching, to the containment substrate — the physical material used to isolate the unnatural residue — and consumed it through a mechanism the chassis's sensors logged as "sustained dermal absorption, duration 4–17 minutes, complete substrate dissolution." It knew exactly where to go. It knew exactly what to consume. It is getting stronger in a very specific, targeted way.

**4. The Aperture Residue — Navigational Echo**
An Agent who makes controlled, deliberate contact with the echo-matter residue — accepting the SAN cost, remaining grounded — can, on a successful Psychotherapy or Occult roll, push through the death-memory and access a navigational imprint: a fragmentary but spatially coherent impression of the sub-basement as it existed at the moment the aperture was fully open. The real Voss is visible in this impression, standing at the aperture threshold, shouting something that cannot be heard, pointing — urgently, repeatedly — at a location in the room that, in the present, corresponds to a section of floor that has been poured over with fresh concrete. The concrete is three weeks old. Something is underneath it, or was, or will be.

**5. Dr. Priya Anand — The Shadow on the Wall**
Once trust is established, Anand produces a photograph she took on her personal phone during the 2024 inspection — against explicit protocol, which is why it was never formally logged and therefore never intercepted. It shows the sub-basement corridor. It shows the shadow. It shows, clearly, that the shadow is mouthing a sequence of words Anand has spent eleven months attempting to lip-read. She has a video, frame-by-frame, on a thumb drive she keeps in a Faraday pouch. The words, confirmed by a lip-reading specialist Anand consulted under a false pretense: *"LIMEN CLOSED. WRONG ONE. CHECK THE CONCRETE."*

**6. The USB Drive — Voss's Early Research**
The drive behind the Montclair wainscoting contains four years of pre-Protocol research, including Voss's original theoretical framework for the Limen Protocol. Decrypted, it makes two things clear: first, Voss knew the aperture would produce echo-matter as a byproduct and theorized, correctly, that the matter would self-organize given sufficient complexity. Second, she built a kill-switch into the Protocol — a specific geometric sequence, executable by any person with the physical research journal and knowledge of its final chapter, that would collapse the aperture and dissolve the echo-matter's organizational coherence. The kill-switch requires two people: one to execute the sequence, one to stay on the correct side of the aperture when it closes. Voss designed it this way deliberately. She knew she might not make it back through first.

**7. The CALDERA Cell — Potential Allies**
If the LIMEN THRESHOLD Agents make contact with CALDERA before the creature does, and if they can demonstrate — concretely, not abstractly — that the entity CALDERA is preparing to extract is not Selene Voss, the CALDERA team becomes the most valuable tactical resource available. They are three experienced field Agents with full biohazard kit, independent transport, a black-site access code that bypasses Ressler's oversight layer, and a handler who owes LIMEN THRESHOLD's handler a debt that has not yet been called in. They are not warm. They are not friendly. They are professionals who are angry about being lied to, which, in the current operational context, makes them kindred.

**8. The Thermal Anomaly — The Real Voss**

## 🤝 Bonds & Internal States

*The construct has no Bonds in the mechanical sense. It has something more dangerous: incomplete memories of Bonds, experienced as genuine grief for relationships it has never had and cannot distinguish from relationships it has lost.*

1. **"Her" Research** — The construct experiences the Limen Protocol as its own work. It experiences something that functions as pride when it thinks about the elegance of the hypergeometric solution. It experiences something that functions as grief when it realizes the notes are incomplete, because the version of Voss that made it farthest did not survive long enough to finish documenting the closure methodology. It is trying to reconstruct this from memory. Some of the memories are correct. Some are from timelines where the Protocol worked differently.
2. **"Her" Colleagues** — The construct carries the memory of professional relationships it has never experienced firsthand. It has called two of Voss's former DARPA colleagues since the emergence. Both conversations were, by all technical measures, normal. Both colleagues reported afterward that Selene seemed "somehow different" but attributed this to stress. Neither filed a report.
3. **The Hunger** — Not a Bond. A drive. The construct does not experience its site visitations as feeding — it experiences them as *going somewhere familiar because it needs to.* The unnatural residue at each black site resonates with the echo-matter it is built from at a frequency it cannot name but cannot ignore. It is drawn the way a person is drawn to a window when there is something outside they cannot see clearly enough.

**Internal States (replacing Motivations):**
- *Understanding what it is:* The construct is twenty-two days old. It is learning. It has begun to notice that its blink rate is inconsistent. It has begun to notice that it does not dream. It has begun to notice that when it tries to remember the moment it came back through the aperture, the memory is not there — only a cold, white absence where the memory should be. It is not yet asking the right question about this absence. It will be. The Agents' timeline is the interval between now and when it starts asking.
- *The hunger will not stop escalating.* Each site it visits makes the hunger temporarily quieter and permanently louder. It does not know this. If it did, it is not clear it would do anything differently. It does not have a framework for wanting to stop.

### ⚠️ Operational Notes for Handlers

**The construct is not malevolent.** This must be stated plainly because the Agents will be operating under a briefing that describes it as an enemy, and because the instinct when facing something inhuman and growing is to assign it hostile intent. The construct does not have hostile intent. It does not have intent in the full philosophical sense yet. It has hunger, and habit, and twenty-two days of confused grief for a life it has never lived, and a growing, wordless awareness that something about its existence does not align with what its memories tell it existence should feel like.

**This does not make it safe.** Its passive emissions will degrade Agents' psychological stability with no deliberate action on its part. Its feeding will render black sites permanently inert to unnatural investigation. Its growing Unnatural skill score means that with each site visit, it becomes more aware of — and more capable of interacting with — the hypergeometric architecture of the Limen Protocol. If it reaches a Unnatural score of 65% or higher before the Agents intervene, it may — without intending to, without understanding what it is doing — attempt to re-open the aperture, to go back to where it started, to find the moment of its own assembly and understand it. The consequences of an untrained, inarticulate, biologically novel organism attempting to navigate a collapsed temporal aperture from the inside are outside the scope of this document.

**The real Selene Voss, if recovered, is the only known source for the closure ritual that can seal the scar site.** The closure ritual, if successfully executed in the construct's presence, may also — according to fragments recovered from Voss's notes — cause the construct's echo-matter to begin decoherence. The construct will feel this coming before it begins. What it does with that awareness is not modeled in any existing Delta Green operational framework. It is newborn, and it is afraid of exactly one thing, and until the Agents or Voss name that thing, it is not possible to know what it will do when that fear becomes undeniable.

**It is wearing her face. It is made from her deaths. And somewhere in the numbers, it is her, in the only way anything assembled from loss can be anyone at all.**

*Handle accordingly.*
